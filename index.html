Research Objective

Physics has long embodied my greatest intellectual curiosity, and as I progressed through my undergraduate journey, I uncovered my strong attraction to computational condensed matter physics. The field is particularly appealing to me as it seamlessly combines theoretical physics, machine learning, and real-world applications. My ultimate goal in pursuing a Ph.D. in Materials Science is to develop cutting-edge solid-state theoretical models and machine learning methods to push the next generation of industrial materials. I am specifically interested in streamlining the discovery and understanding of quantum materials, which possess real-world impacts in sustainable energy, optoelectronics, and ultra-sensitive measurements.
